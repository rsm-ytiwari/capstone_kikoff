The Three Datasets & How They Work Together

Spend Dataset (11,506 rows) - Daily media spend by channel and platform - Role: Model inputs (the X variables — what we control) - Quality: Considered accurate; starting point for budget optimization

Conversions + LTV Dataset (1,193 rows) - Daily conversions and predicted lifetime value - Role: Model outputs/targets (the Y variables — what we’re trying to predict) - Quality: Model-based, not realized; missing 2 dates

Incrementality Tests (4 tests) - Experimental results from holdout/geo tests - Role: Ground truth for calibration (constraints on model parameters) - Quality: 3 clean, 1 compromised; used to validate MMM estimates

The workflow: 1. Merge spend + conversions/LTV into daily table 2. Fit preliminary MMM (estimate channel effects from correlations) 3. Compare MMM-implied CAC against experimentally-measured iCAC 4. Adjust priors/coefficients so MMM respects the experimental evidence

Key EDA Findings

1. Spend concentration - Top 3 channels dominate total budget - Long tail of smaller channels and tactics

2. Adstock signal confirmed - 7-21 day lag between spend and conversions - Indicates advertising carryover effect - Adstock transformation required in model

3. Tax season seasonality - January–April peak in conversions (tax filing season) - Baseline ~2,000 conversions/day; peak ~3,000+ on high days - Model must include explicit seasonal component

4. Revenue signal stronger than volume - 7-day lagged correlation (spend vs. revenue) > spend vs. conversions alone - Suggests LTV varies with timing/channel - Supports revenue-based optimization target

5. Day-of-week pattern exists - Weekday vs. weekend differences - Relevant for daily-level modeling granularity

Blocking Decisions 2 & 3

Decision 2: Platform Data Handling

Current state: Spend data contains iOS, Android, Web, plus some combined rows.

Option A: Disaggregate - Model each channel × platform separately (iOS-TikTok, Android-TikTok, Web-TikTok, etc.) - Pros: Platform-specific insights, different response curves per platform - Cons: ~3× more parameters; requires more data per segment; risk of overfitting

Option B: Aggregate to channel level - Pool all platforms per channel - Pros: Simpler, more stable estimates, less data fragmentation - Cons: Lose platform-specific insights; can’t optimize budgets separately by platform

Supervisor input: Which approach fits your use case?

Decision 3: Primary Optimization Target

If selecting revenue: Do you want 1-year LTV horizon or 3-year?

Why it matters: This becomes the model’s objective function. All ROI and contribution estimates derive from this choice.
