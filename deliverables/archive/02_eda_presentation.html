Critical Decisions for Phase 2

Required Input Summary

Three decisions are required before Phase 2 can proceed. All three are blocking and shape the model architecture.

1. Channel Taxonomy Mapping (Blocking)

Requirement: Map each SOURCE_GROUP value to your business channel taxonomy.

Impact: Determines model parameter space, incrementality prior assignment, and all downstream reporting.

Input needed: - Mapping table (SOURCE_GROUP → business channel) - Clarification on whether programmatic DSP vendors (applovin, liftoff, appvertiser) are separate channels or should be grouped

2. Platform Data Handling (Blocking)

Requirement: Clarify approach for combined platform values (“web and app”, “iOS and android”).

Impact: Determines model granularity and parameter space dimensionality.

Decision options: - Disaggregate combined rows and model at channel-platform level (increases parameters by ~3x) - Aggregate all platform rows to channel level (simpler, loses platform-specific insights)

3. Primary Optimization Target (Blocking)

Requirement: Confirm primary variable for model calibration and budget optimization.

Impact: Shapes model objective function and all ROI/contribution estimates.

Decision options: - Purchase volume (CONVERSIONS) - Customer lifetime value (LTV_1YEAR) - Total revenue (CONVERSIONS × LTV)

If choosing revenue: confirm 1-year vs. 3-year LTV horizon.

4. Test Data Quality (Important)

Requirement: Clarify status of January 2026 Meta incrementality test (cancelled, approximate spend).

Impact: Affects prior uncertainty and Meta channel calibration strategy.

Question: Include with high-uncertainty prior or exclude from calibration?

5. LTV Anomalies (Important)

Requirement: Identify remaining ~8 bad LTV values beyond the 2 identified nulls.

Impact: Data preprocessing step; affects time series stability.

Input needed: List of dates or identification rule (threshold range, statistical criteria, etc.)
