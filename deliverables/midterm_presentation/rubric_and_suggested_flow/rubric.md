## Group Assessment Rubric

| **Criterion** | **Strongly Agree (5)** | **Agree (4)** | **Somewhat Agree (3)** | **Disagree (2)** | **Strongly Disagree (1)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Students have a clear understanding of the business, the role, the problem, and the outcomes/metrics.** | Clearly and precisely articulate the business problem, context, significance, and implications. | Clearly identify the business problem and context, with minor gaps in explaining significance. | Adequately identify the main problem but may omit relevant context or implications. | Vaguely identify the problem; context or significance unclear. | Incorrect or unclear identification; fundamental misunderstanding evident. |
| **The quality of the exploratory data analysis in the presentation is very high.** | Comprehensive, insightful summary of data with clear evaluation of its quality, completeness, and limitations. | Clear summary of data with relevant details on quality and limitations, minor gaps exist. | Basic summary of data, adequate mention of quality, some important details missing. | Partial or superficial summary, unclear or incomplete discussion of quality issues. | Ineffective or inaccurate summary; major misunderstandings regarding data quality. |
| **The slides do not use coding snapshots at all and are not text heavy.** | Slides contain no coding snapshots and are concise and visually appealing with minimal text. | Slides contain no coding snapshots and are generally concise, though some slides could still reduce text further. | Slides contain no coding snapshots but have a moderate amount of text, occasionally feeling dense or wordy. | Slides contain no coding snapshots but are frequently text-heavy and somewhat cluttered. | Slides either contain coding snapshots or are consistently text-heavy, cluttered, and difficult to quickly grasp. |
| **The visuals/graphics and charts are well-formatted — they use the right scale, legends and number of decimal places.** | Graphics and charts are perfectly formatted, scales are appropriate, legends clear and informative, decimal places consistently applied and appropriate for clarity and precision. | Graphics and charts formatted well with minor inconsistencies; scales mostly accurate, legends clear, and decimal places appropriate. | Formatting generally acceptable, though minor errors in scales, legends, or decimal places affect clarity or accuracy. | Significant formatting issues; scales unclear or inappropriate, legends missing or confusing, decimal places inconsistent or misleading. | Graphics and charts poorly formatted; major errors in scales, legends missing or incorrect, decimal usage inappropriate or severely misleading. |
| **The heading/title of each slide is a takeaway from the slide and not merely a description of slide contents.** | All headings clearly present a meaningful takeaway or key insight from the slide content, going beyond mere descriptions. | Most headings provide clear takeaways or insights, though a few may be somewhat descriptive rather than insightful. | Headings are occasionally phrased as clear takeaways, but many remain primarily descriptive. | Most headings merely describe slide content, lacking a clear takeaway or insight. | Headings consistently fail to present takeaways; they solely describe slide contents and do not highlight key messages or insights. |
| **The analysis is credible and relevant.** | Solution clearly and logically aligns with the underlying goal; explicitly demonstrates how each step supports achieving objectives, with excellent clarity and rationale. | Solution aligns well with the underlying goal, with clear connections between steps and objectives, though minor clarifications could enhance the rationale. | Solution generally connects to the goal adequately; some aspects may lack clear explanation or direct relevance. | Solution shows weak alignment with the goal; connections unclear or inconsistently explained, raising doubts about relevance. | Solution is disconnected or poorly aligned with the underlying goal, indicating fundamental misunderstandings or irrelevance. |
| **There is a brief discussion of alternative methods that the team is considering.** | Presenters explicitly and succinctly discuss multiple alternative methods considered, clearly explaining why each was ultimately rejected. | Presenters briefly discuss several alternatives considered, providing reasonable explanations for their choices, though explanations could be slightly clearer or more concise. | Presenters mention at least one alternative method considered but offer limited explanation of why it was not chosen. | Presenters only superficially acknowledge that alternatives were considered, without explaining what these alternatives were or why they were rejected. | Presenters do not mention alternative methods or any decision-making process behind choosing the final solution. |
| **The conclusion provides a clear and well-structured summary of all major progress to date.** | Provides a clear, concise, and well-structured summary of all major progress to date. Accurately describes data collection, preparation, and key preliminary analyses. The audience can easily understand what has been accomplished and how it advances the project goals. | Summarizes most key progress areas with clarity. Minor gaps in detail or structure, but the main accomplishments are understandable and connected to project goals. | Provides a basic overview of progress-to-date but lacks depth or specificity. Some important work is mentioned only briefly or left out. Audience may need to infer connections to goals. | Progress summary is incomplete, vague, or poorly organized. Omits major accomplishments or provides little sense of how the work so far advances the project. | Fails to summarize progress-to-date or does so inaccurately. Audience is left unclear about what has been done and how the project is moving forward. |
| **The calls to action/next steps for the project are clear.** | Calls to action and next steps are exceptionally clear, practical, actionable, and explicitly detailed, leaving no ambiguity about implementation. | Clearly articulated calls to action and next steps, practical and mostly actionable, with minor clarifications needed. | Adequate clarity on calls to action/next steps, generally actionable, though some steps may lack specificity or clarity. | Calls to action or next steps are unclear or vaguely presented, creating uncertainty about practical implementation. | Calls to action or next steps are absent, unclear, impractical, or irrelevant, providing no clear guidance for implementation. |
| **The flow, pace, and transitions were smooth and well-executed.** | Presentation flow is exceptionally smooth and logical, with well-paced delivery and seamless, engaging transitions between sections. | Presentation has clear, logical flow; pace is appropriate with well-executed transitions, minor refinements could enhance engagement. | Adequate flow and pace with acceptable transitions; occasional awkwardness or abrupt transitions may slightly disrupt engagement. | Flow or pace uneven; transitions frequently abrupt, unclear, or poorly managed, disrupting overall coherence. | Poor flow and pace, severely impacting clarity; transitions missing, confusing, or ineffective, significantly detracting from the presentation's coherence. |
| **The analysis would be readily understood by a non-technical audience.** | Analysis presented with exceptional clarity, easily comprehensible to a non-technical audience; uses simple language, clearly explains concepts, avoids unnecessary jargon, and clearly communicates key insights. | Analysis clearly understandable by a non-technical audience; occasional use of technical language adequately clarified, minor simplifications could improve understanding. | Analysis is generally understandable, but moderate reliance on technical terms or explanations occasionally reduces clarity for non-technical listeners. | Analysis difficult to understand; excessive technical language or insufficient explanations frequently confuse a non-technical audience. | Analysis incomprehensible to a non-technical audience; extensive jargon, lack of clarity, explanations missing or severely inadequate. |
| **The presentation appeared well-rehearsed.** | Presentation is highly polished and confidently delivered; strong evidence of thorough rehearsal, seamless coordination among presenters, and clear familiarity with content. | Presentation delivered confidently with clear preparation evident; minor lapses in coordination or fluidity suggest slight room for improvement. | Adequately rehearsed, with basic preparation evident; occasional hesitation, awkwardness, or coordination issues noticeable. | Presentation suggests limited rehearsal; frequent hesitations, unclear roles, or awkward pauses disrupt the delivery. | Presentation appears unrehearsed; significant confusion, frequent pauses, and evident lack of coordination significantly undermine effectiveness. |
